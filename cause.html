Cause

Burning fossil fuels

This includes burning coal, oil, and natural gas for electricity, heat, and industrial processes, which is a major source of carbon dioxide emissions.

Deforestation

When forests are cleared for other uses, the trees release the carbon they have stored, and there are fewer trees to absorb carbon dioxide from the atmosphere.

Industrial processes

Manufacturing goods, such as cement, steel, and plastics, requires significant energy, often from burning fossil fuels, and industrial processes themselves can release greenhouse gases.

Agriculture & Farming activities

Livestock produce methane, fertilizers release nitrous oxide, and land clearing for crops and grazing all contribute to greenhouse gas emissions.

Transportation

The combustion of fossil fuels in cars, trucks, ships, and airplanes is a major contributor to greenhouse gas emissions.
