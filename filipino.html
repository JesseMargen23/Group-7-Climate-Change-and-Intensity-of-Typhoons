Climate Change and Intensity of Typhoons

Climate change has made typhoons in the Philippines stronger and more destructive, causing heavier rainfall, hurricanes and flooding. According to PAGASA (the Philippine Atmospheric Geophysical and Astronomical Services Administration) an increasing number of super typhoons entering Philippines weather zone has gone up to 50% and disasters like Typhoon Yolanda in 2013 have caused over 6,000 deaths and billions worth in damages. Rising sea temperatures continue to constantly create more intense storms that greatly impact Filipino communities, especially those in coastal areas.
