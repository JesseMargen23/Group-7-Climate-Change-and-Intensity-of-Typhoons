Call to action

Ethical Implications

Climate change creates stronger and dangerous typhoons, and as global citizens, we have the responsibility to reduce the harm that we cause to the environment, like waste management, flood control and global warming. Everyone has a duty to take part in actions that lower waste, protect vulnerable communities and support policies that address climate change and to lessen them.

Five suggestions for actionable steps that individuals can take:

1. Reduce waste by recycling, reusing materials, and limiting single-use plastics.

2. Stay informed about typhoon warnings. Monitoring PAGASA alerts helps individuals prepare early and reduce the impact of severe typhoons.

3. Prepare an emergency kit that have essentials like first-aid, water, and flashlights that helps families stay safe when strong typhoons hit.

4. Support flood-control projects because it help improve flood protection and clean canals in your community

5. Reduce carbon emissions at home like turning off unused appliances, save electricity, and minimize activities that contribute to global warming.
