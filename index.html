The Climate-Typhoon Connection

Understanding why storms are getting stronger and more destructive.

Overview: The Global Issue

Climate change, fueled by excess greenhouse gases, is dangerously heating the planet's oceans. These warmer sea surface temperatures provide **more energy** and moisture, acting like a supercharger that rapidly intensifies tropical cyclones, leading to higher wind speeds and more devastating rainfall. This phenomenon is directly connected to globalization, as interconnected global supply chains and massive industrialization drive the continuous increase in carbon emissions worldwide. This economic structure accelerates the warming, while also making densely populated, globally-linked coastal cities (like those in Asia) incredibly vulnerable to catastrophic economic and human loss.
